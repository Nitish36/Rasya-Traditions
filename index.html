The Art of the Perfect Drape

Small secrets that make a big difference in your saree look.

The Height Rule

Always put on your heels before you start draping. This ensures the bottom length is perfectly aligned with the floor.

üìè

The Pallu Length

For an elegant, taller silhouette, ensure your Pallu falls just below the back of your knee. Too short makes it look bulky.

üß∑

Invisible Pinning

Always pin your shoulder pleats from the inside of the blouse. This hides the pin and gives a seamless, clean finish.

üìê

The Center Tuck

Start your first tuck exactly at the center of your waist. This keeps the saree borders aligned and prevents it from shifting.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the pre-pleating process take? +

Typically, it takes 24 to 48 hours depending on the fabric and the number of sarees. For urgent bridal requirements, please contact us in advance for express service.

Do I need to provide my own safety pins? +

No, you don't! We use high-quality, rust-proof safety pins that are fabric-friendly to ensure your saree stays secure without any damage.

Can you handle heavy bridal Kanchipuram or Banarasi sarees? +

Yes, we specialize in heavy silk sarees. We use specialized steam ironing and traditional folding techniques (like the Silk-Care Fold) to preserve the zari and embroidery.

Do you offer home pickup and delivery? +

We currently offer pickup and delivery within the city. for orders of 3 or more sarees. You can also drop them off at our studio.

Saree Care Tips

Preserving your heirlooms for generations to come.

üå¨Ô∏è

Let It Breathe

Avoid plastic covers. Always store your silk sarees in breathable cotton or muslin bags to prevent moisture buildup and mildew.

üîÑ

Change the Folds

Every 3-4 months, take your sarees out and refold them along different lines. This prevents the fabric from tearing at the creases.

üö´

No Direct Perfume

Never spray perfume or deodorant directly on the saree. The chemicals can cause permanent staining or weaken the delicate zari threads.

üëî

Hanger Warning

Never leave heavy silk sarees on metal hangers for too long. The weight can stretch the fabric and distort the silhouette.

üí° Pro Tip: If your saree has real silver/gold zari, wrap it in acid-free tissue paper to prevent the metal from tarnishing!
